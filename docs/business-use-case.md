# Business Use Case: Asset, Preventive Maintenance, and Work Order Lifecycle in IBM Maximo Application Suite 8.x

## 1. Business Overview

This document outlines the business context for managing assets, preventive maintenance (PM), and work orders WS in IBM Maximo Application Suite 8.x (MAS). It focuses on the needs of aset-intensive operators (eg.g. manufacturing, energy, utilities, public sector) seeking to reduce downtime, optimize maintenance costs, and improve regulatory compliance.

## 2. Current Maintenance Challenges

### 2.1 Operational Context

- Large and diverse asset portfolio (production lines, utilities, field equipment)
- Mixed age of assets from new installations to legacy intrastructure
- Strict availability requirements (throughboxing and audit trails, regulated inspections)
- Multiple maintenance teams and contractors working across sites and systems 

### 2.2 Key Maintenance Issues 

- ## Inconsistent asset registration and lifecycle tracking
  - Asset inventories and status are tracked in multiple spreadsheets, excel sheets, and local spreadsheets
  - No single source of truth for complete lifecycle data
- ## Inadequate preventive maintenance cuverage
  - PM activities are genralized, adhoc-based, or manually tracked
  - Some systems are corrective-only, leading to higher rates of breakdown and unavailable downtime
- ## Limœ visibility of work backlog
  - Engineering and operations managers cannot easily answer questions about work status
  - Work orders are created in email and excel and never closed-looped into a centralized system
- ## Compliance and risk management
  - Documentation of statutory or legislated verification for EHL and other regulatory requirements is time-consuming
  - Risk asset tasks have higher guardian requirements (grid outages, SLAS, calibration) that are not factored into the daily work planning

- ## Cost and downtime related challenges
  - Inconsistency hetween operations, finance, and maintenance teams on scope, in part due to poor insight
  - Difficulty identifying assets and work orders that cause high downtime or contractual slippedages

### 2.3 Business Goals

- Reduce unplanned downtime and prodong failures
- Maintain asset availability and safety
- Improve compliance with engineering and regulatory requirements
- Optimize maintenance resource utilisation (in-house teams, contractors, spares)
- Drive data-driven improvements in maintenance strategy, asset planning, and capital budgeting.
